Common Parameters
Core Formulas
Gear Ratio i = Z₂ / Z₁ = N₁ / N₂ Pitch Dia d = m × Z (mm) Centre Dist a = m × (Z₁ + Z₂) / 2 (mm) Output Speed N₂ = N₁ / i (RPM) Output Torque T₂ = T₁ × i × η
m = module (tooth size standard). Z = number of teeth. η = efficiency fraction.
Tooth Geometry
Addendum a = 1.00 × m (tip above pitch circle) Dedendum b = 1.25 × m (root below pitch circle) Whole Height h = 2.25 × m (total tooth height) Tooth Thickness t = π × m / 2 (arc thickness at pitch circle) Face Width B = k × m (k = 8–14 typical) Clearance c = 0.25 × m (gap between tip and root) Tip Diameter da = m × (Z + 2) Root Diameter df = m × (Z − 2.5) Base Circle db = d × cos(φ) (φ = pressure angle)
Face width B determines load capacity. Wider face = more contact area = higher torque capacity.
Contact Ratio
ε = [√(da1²/4 − db1²/4) + √(da2²/4 − db2²/4) − a×sin(φ)] / (π×m×cos(φ)) Target: ε > 1.2 (smooth transfer between teeth) Typical: ε = 1.4–1.8 for standard spur gears
Contact ratio > 1 means multiple teeth share load at all times. Higher = quieter and stronger.

Helical Gear Formulas
Transverse Module mt = mₙ / cos(ψ) Transverse Pitch pt = π × mt Axial Pitch px = π × mₙ / tan(ψ) Pitch Diameter d = mt × Z Centre Distance a = mt × (Z₁ + Z₂) / 2 Transverse PA φt = atan(tan(φₙ) / cos(ψ)) Tooth Height: Addendum a = 1.00 × mₙ Dedendum b = 1.25 × mₙ Whole Height h = 2.25 × mₙ Normal thickness tₙ = π × mₙ / 2 Face Width B = k × mₙ (k = 8–20) Forces: Tangential Wt = 2T / d Axial Wa = Wt × tan(ψ) Radial Wr = Wt × tan(φₙ) / cos(ψ) Overlap Ratio εβ = B × tan(ψ) / (π × mₙ)

Internal Gear Formulas
Gear Ratio i = Zᵣ / Zₚ Centre Distance a = m × (Zᵣ − Zₚ) / 2 ← subtract, not add! Planetary Gear Formulas
Mesh Condition Zr = Zs + 2 × Zp Gear Ratio i = 1 + Zr/Zs (ring fixed, sun→carrier) Output RPM Nc = N_sun / i Output Torque Tc = T_sun × i × η Tooth Height (all gears use same module): Addendum a = 1.00 × m Dedendum b = 1.25 × m Height h = 2.25 × m Thickness t = π × m / 2 Face Width B = k × m Assembly Checks: 1. Mesh Zr = Zs + 2Zp (must be exact integer) 2. Spacing (Zs + Zr) mod Np = 0 (planets must fit equally) 3. Clearance 2 sin(π/Np) × Ac > dp (planets must not touch) 4. Undercut df_sun > 0, df_planet > 0 Configurations (change which member is fixed): Ring fixed, Sun in → Carrier out i = 1 + Zr/Zs Sun fixed, Ring in → Carrier out i = 1 + Zs/Zr Carrier fix,Sun in → Ring out i = −Zr/Zs (reversal)
Multiple planets share the load — typically 3 planets give 3× the torque capacity of a single planet. Coaxial in/out allows compact inline designs.

Spiral Bevel Formulas

Worm Gear Formulas — BS 721-2 / IS:3734 / AGMA 6022
GIVEN: m, z₁ (starts), z₂ (wheel teeth), q (diameter factor), αₙ=20° STEP 1 — Axial Pitch: px = π × m STEP 2 — Lead: L = px × z₁ STEP 3 — Lead Angle: γ = arctan(z₁ / q) [BS 721-2] cos γ = cos(γ) [needed for steps 4,6,7] STEP 4 — Axial Pressure Angle: tan(αx) = tan(αₙ) / cos(γ) [BS 721-2] STEP 5 — Worm PCD: d₁ = q × m STEP 6 — Addendum (SAME for both): ha = 1.000 × m [BS 721-2 §3.2] STEP 7 — Clearance (in wheel root only): c = 0.20 × m × cos(γ) [BS 721-2 §3.4] STEP 8 — Worm Dedendum: hf₁ = m × (2.2 × cos(γ) − 1) [BS 721-2 §3.3] STEP 9 — Wheel Dedendum: hf₂ = hf₁ + c [DIFFERENT from hf₁] STEP 10 — Total Heights: ht₁ = ha + hf₁ (worm) ht₂ = ha + hf₂ (wheel, TALLER by c) STEP 11 — Working Depth: hw = 2 × m STEP 12 — Worm Diameters: da₁ = d₁ + 2 × ha (outside) df₁ = d₁ − 2 × hf₁ (root) STEP 13 — Wheel PCD: d₂ = m × z₂ STEP 14 — Wheel Diameters: da₂ = d₂ + 2 × ha (throat) df₂ = d₂ − 2 × hf₂ (root) de₂ = da₂ + 2 × m (rim) STEP 15 — Centre Distance: C = (d₁ + d₂) / 2 STEP 16 — Tooth Widths: Pitch width (SAME): t = px / 2 Tip width (SAME): tt = t − 2 × ha × tan(αx) Worm root width: tr₁ = t + 2 × hf₁ × tan(αx) Wheel root width: tr₂ = t + 2 × hf₂ × tan(αx) [DIFFERENT] Normal thickness: tn = t × cos(γ) STEP 17 — Backlash (IS:3734 Table 3): j per module grade Actual thickness: ta = t − j/2 STEP 18 — Chordal (Vernier): Worm chord: tch₁ = d₁ × sin(t/d₁) Wheel chord: tch₂ = d₂ × sin(t/d₂) Worm jaw height: ach₁ = ha + t²/(4×d₁) Wheel jaw height: ach₂ = ha + t²/(4×d₂) STEP 19 — Efficiency: η = tan(γ) / tan(γ + ρ) where ρ = arctan(μ/cos(αₙ)) [friction angle] STEP 20 — Self-Locking: when γ < ρ (lead angle < friction angle)
⚠ KEY: Worm and wheel dedendums are NOT equal. hf₁ ≠ hf₂. Clearance c lives entirely in the wheel root. This means ht₁ ≠ ht₂ and root widths differ. Never use 1.25×m for both — that is the common error. Standard: BS 721-2:1983, IS:3734, ISO 1122-1.

Non-Circular Gear Formulas (Elliptical)
Ellipse equation: r(θ) = a(1−e²) / (1 + e×cos(θ)) Eccentricity: e = √(1 − (b/a)²) Instant ratio: i(θ) = r₁(θ) / r₂(θ) Constraint: r₁(θ) + r₂(θ) = C (constant centre dist) Radius extremes: r_min = a(1−e), r_max = a(1+e) Ratio range: i_max = r_max/r_min, i_min = r_min/r_max Tooth Height (use at mean radius): Module m = pitch / π (at design point) Addendum a = 1.00 × m Dedendum b = 1.25 × m Height h = 2.25 × m Thickness t = π × m / 2 Face Width B = k × m

Gear Ratio i = Z₂ / Z₁ Pitch Diameter d = m × Z Centre Distance a = m × (Z₁ + Z₂) / 2 (exact centre distance CRITICAL) Cycloid Profile (epicycloid on addendum, hypocycloid on dedendum): Epicycloid: x = (R+r)cos(t) − r×cos((R+r)t/r) y = (R+r)sin(t) − r×sin((R+r)t/r) Hypocycloid:x = (R−r)cos(t) + r×cos((R−r)t/r) y = (R−r)sin(t) − r×sin((R−r)t/r) R = pitch circle radius, r = rolling circle radius Tooth Height: Addendum a = r (rolling circle radius for addendum) Dedendum b = r + 0.1×m (rolling circle + clearance) Height h = 2r + 0.1×m Face Width B = k × m Pressure angle varies from 0° at pitch point — NOT constant like involute.
Cycloidal gears require precise centre distance — unlike involute gears which tolerate small centre distance errors. Used in clocks because very small tooth counts (even 3 teeth) work without undercutting.

Harmonic Drive Formulas
Gear Ratio i = Zc / (Zc − Zf) (circular spline fixed, flex spline = output) Tooth Difference ΔZ = Zc − Zf = 2 (standard — must be 2) Tooth Height (very fine teeth, usually m = 0.2–1.0): Addendum a = 1.00 × m Dedendum b = 1.00 × m (reduced — flex spline must bend) Height h = 2.00 × m (shorter than standard for flexibility) Thickness t = π × m / 2 Tip clearance = 0.05 × m (tighter than standard) Flex Spline Deformation: Radial deflection δ = d / (2 × i) (approx wave generator oval) Bending stress must be checked for fatigue (flex spline cycles every rev) Backlash ≈ 0–3 arc-minutes (vs 5–30 for conventional gears) Efficiency η = 65–90% (lower at high ratios due to flex losses)
ΔZ = 2 means the flex spline has 2 fewer teeth than circular spline — each wave generator revolution advances output by 2 teeth. Zero backlash makes harmonic drives essential for robot joints requiring precise positioning.

Geneva Mechanism — Intermittent motion drive — converts continuous rotation into precise indexed steps. Used in watches, film projectors, and automated machinery.

1. Centre distance a = r / sin(π/n) 2. Lock radius R = √(a² − r²) 3. Slot depth d_slot = R + r_pin + 0.5mm clearance 4. Engagement angle α = π/2 − π/n [rad] 5. Index angle/cycle θ = 360°/n 6. Dwell angle β_dwl = 360° − 2α·(180/π) 7. Index time t_i = (2α/2π) × (60/RPM) 8. Dwell time t_d = (1 − 2α/2π) × (60/RPM) 9. Max wheel ω ω_max = ω·r·a·cos(α) / (a²+r²−2ar·cos α) 10. Wheel diameter D_w = 2·(R + r_pin + 2mm)
Entry condition: a·sin(α) = r (crank ⊥ slot at engagement). Minimum n=3 (120° index). Locking disc prevents reverse during dwell.
Slot Count: Minimum n=3 (120° index), most common n=4 (90°). Higher n gives finer indexing but longer dwell ratio.
Entry Condition: Pin must enter slot tangentially — crank and slot centreline must be perpendicular at entry. This is guaranteed by the geometry: a·sin(α) = r.
Locking Disc: A concentric locking disc on the driver engages a circular lock on the wheel during dwell. Clearance ≈ 0.05–0.1 mm.
Shock Loading: Geneva mechanisms produce jerk at entry/exit. Use hardened pins (60 HRC). Service factor Ks ≥ 1.25 recommended.
Pin Diameter: Typically d_pin = 0.1·a to 0.15·a. Slot width = d_pin + 0.05 mm clearance.
IS 9001 Application: Geneva mechanisms are used as index drives in automatic machinery, film projectors, and rotary tables.

Ratchet & Pawl Equations
Tooth Pitch: p = π × d / Z Tangential Force: F_t = 2T / d (× SF for design) Force per Pawl: F_p = F_t / n_pawls Tooth Bearing Stress: σ_br = F_p / (b × h) ≤ σ_allow_bearing Pawl Shear Stress: τ = F_p / (w_p × b) ≤ τ_allow Pawl Bending Stress: σ_b = 6×F_p×l_p / (w_p²×b) ≤ σ_allow_bend Backlash: δ = 360° / (Z × n_pawls) No-slip condition: α < arctan(μ) [μ ≈ 0.15 dry steel] Factor of Safety: FOS = min(σ_allow/σ_act, τ_allow/τ_act)
Ratchet angle α = 10°–30°. Self-locking requires α < friction angle φ = arctan(μ). For μ=0.15, φ ≈ 8.5° — most ratchet teeth are not self-locking by friction alone; the pawl spring provides engagement force.
Ratchet Angle: α = 10°–30°. Smaller angle provides finer indexing but reduces tooth strength. 15° is typical for general-purpose ratchets.
No Back-Slip: α must be less than friction angle φ = arctan(μ). For μ=0.15, φ≈8.5°. Most ratchets rely on spring-loaded pawl engagement, not geometry alone.
Multiple Pawls: Reduce angular backlash. 2 pawls at 180° offset: backlash = 360°/2Z. 3 pawls at 120° offset: backlash = 360°/3Z.
Pawl Spring: Must provide adequate engagement force but not cause excessive noise at high speed. Typical spring force = 5–15% of tangential tooth force.
Surface Treatment: Use hardened pawl tip (58–62 HRC) to resist impact loading on each engagement cycle.
Tooth Height: h ≈ p/2.5 is standard. Larger h increases strength but reduces tooth count for a given diameter.
SF ≥ 3.0: Use for ratchets due to the impact nature of loading on each engagement cycle.
Contact Stress: Check pawl tip for Hertz contact stress at the ratchet tooth flank contact point using cylindrical contact formula.
